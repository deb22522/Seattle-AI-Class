Course Description

This course provides pre-service educators with comprehensive knowledge of federal and state laws governing special education services. Students will examine the historical development, current provisions, and practical applications of legislation affecting students with disabilities, their families, and educational professionals.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  • Analyze the historical evolution of special education law and its impact on current practice
  • Demonstrate understanding of IDEA, Section 504, and ADA provisions
  • Evaluate IEP and 504 plan development processes and legal requirements
  • Apply procedural safeguards and due process protections in educational settings
  • Examine discipline policies and procedures for students with disabilities
  • Assess transition planning requirements and post-secondary implications
  • Identify professional responsibilities and liability issues for educators

Required Texts

  • Yell, M. L. (2019). The Law and Special Education (5th ed.). Pearson
  • Selected court cases and federal regulations (provided via course management system)

Course Schedule

Week 1-2: Historical Foundations

  • Evolution from exclusion to inclusion
  • Landmark court cases: PARC v. Pennsylvania, Mills v. Board of Education
  • Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act (1973)

Week 3-4: IDEA Fundamentals

  • Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE)
  • Least Restrictive Environment (LRE)
  • Child Find obligations
  • Eligibility categories and evaluation procedures

Week 5-6: IEP Development and Implementation

  • IEP team composition and responsibilities
  • Required IEP components
  • Annual reviews and three-year reevaluations
  • IEP meeting procedures and parent participation

Week 7-8: Procedural Safeguards

  • Prior written notice requirements
  • Informed consent provisions
  • Due process hearing procedures
  • Mediation and resolution sessions

Week 9-10: Discipline and Behavioral Interventions

  • Manifestation determination reviews
  • Interim alternative educational settings
  • Functional behavioral assessments
  • Positive behavioral supports

Week 11-12: Transition Services

  • Age-appropriate transition assessments
  • Post-secondary goals and services
  • Interagency collaboration
  • Transfer of rights at age of majority

Week 13-14: Related Services and Accommodations

  • Transportation, assistive technology, and therapeutic services
  • Section 504 plans vs. IEPs
  • Reasonable accommodations in general education
  • Assessment accommodations and modifications

Week 15: Professional Responsibilities

  • Confidentiality and FERPA compliance
  • Educator liability and immunity
  • Advocacy and ethical considerations

Assignments and Assessment

25%

Case Study Analysis

Students will analyze a complex special education case, identifying legal issues and proposing solutions based on current law.

20%

Legal Brief Portfolio

Collection of summaries for 10 significant special education court cases, with analysis of implications for educational practice.

20%

IEP Development Project

Collaborative development of a legally compliant IEP, including all required components and documentation.

15%

Midterm Examination

Comprehensive exam covering IDEA provisions, procedural safeguards, and case law through Week 8.

20%

Final Examination

Cumulative final exam emphasizing application of legal principles to educational scenarios.
